2 Displacmeent#

  • \(\pi\) Fate. The concept of heritage is indeed anchored in the continuity of place, community, and time. Our ancestors, rooted in their stable environments, passed down traditions, values, and practices that were cultivated over generations. They lived in a world where the community was often defined by a specific location, shared history, and a predictable rhythm of life. Iran has persian, islamic, and western influences, akin to three probability density functions with limited overlap.   • \(g(t)\) Subdistribution. However, the dynamics of displacement—whether through emigration, immigration, or diaspora—introduce complexity to this stability. These experiences disrupt the continuity of heritage, leading to the intermingling of cultures, adaptation to new environments, and the evolution of identities. Displacement forces individuals and communities to redefine their heritage in a new context, often blending old traditions with new influences. This process can be both enriching and challenging, as it requires a balance between preserving one’s roots and embracing new experiences.

  • \( h_k(t) = \lim_{\Delta t \to 0} \frac{P(t \leq T < t + \Delta t, \text{event} = k \mid T \geq t)}{\Delta t}\) Cause-specific. Music, with its ii-V7-i progression, beautifully encapsulates this idea. The progression is a journey: starting from a point of tension (ii), moving through a dominant resolution (V7), and finally arriving at a place of rest (i). This musical journey mirrors the experience of displacement and diaspora. The tension of leaving one's homeland, the dominant forces of adaptation and integration, and the eventual resolution in finding a new sense of belonging and identity. In the context of disjointed times and the diaspora, our heritage becomes a living, breathing entity—constantly evolving, yet deeply connected to its origins. It’s a testament to the resilience and adaptability of human culture, demonstrating how we can create harmony and meaning even amidst the most profound changes.

AM#

The diminished seventh chord (dim7) is inherently chromatic due to its structure. A dim7 chord is made up of minor third intervals stacked on top of each other, creating a symmetrical, equal-interval configuration that does not fit neatly into the diatonic scale of any key. This symmetry makes it a versatile and intriguing chord that can resolve in various ways, often used to modulate between keys or create tension that demands resolution. Its chromatic nature allows it to function effectively as a passing chord or to add dramatic tension in both classical and jazz music.

What brought me here was BWV 1068 II. The opening 4 bars are serene and mostly at “home” on the root chord of I, but with inversions in base: root, 3rd inversion, vi7 (which is root/vi), 2nd inversion etc. then comes the 5th bar!!! All that was serene, stepwise, Ionian, is now thrown into disarray… paradise lost (we can link Milton to Dante). 1st inversion of the root quickly gives way to another mode (iidim7 flirting with the Dorian, then V7b9 doubling-down on bended-knee in a Dorian proposal: initially in 1st inversion [and a motif Chopin lifted from here to Op 28 No 4 in the same mode & key] but then in root, before leading to i of the Dorian… which is E minor). the E minor then changes function to the ii of the Ionian (two-become-one), then we have the V7 of the Ionian and led back to paradiso I… this, I guess, appeals to me more in terms of employing traditional aesthetics. Chopins employed these traditional aesthetics founded on a ii-V7-I with modal interchange on which to build fractals of this motif.

5 Cumulative#

Bach’s BWV 1068 (Air on the G String)#

  • Harmonic Progression: The serene, stable opening in the Ionian mode establishes a strong sense of home. The subtle use of inversions adds richness without disturbing the calm.

  • Shift to Uncertainty: The transition into the Dorian mode introduces a layer of complexity and uncertainty. This shift is not abrupt but seamless, showcasing Bach’s genius in modulation and voice leading.

  • Resolution: The return to the Ionian mode after exploring the Dorian mode provides a satisfying resolution. This journey from stability to complexity and back to stability can be deeply satisfying, reflecting a traditional aesthetic that values balance and resolution.

Comparison with “Clair de Lune”#

  • “Clair de Lune”: Debussy’s piece is less about harmonic stability and more about creating an atmospheric, impressionistic soundscape. The harmonic language is fluid, often avoiding strong resolutions in favor of ambiguity and color.

  • Emotional Journey: While “Clair de Lune” evokes a serene and reflective mood, it does so with a sense of fluidity and impermanence. In contrast, BWV 1068 offers a more structured emotional journey, from serenity through complexity back to serenity, which might feel more grounded and satisfying if you prefer traditional harmonic progressions.

  • Impressionists: Are these mostly formless emotional modes of imitation works of charity? Are they able to lend support to future artists as “cumulative wisdom”? Endurance, resilience, hardening? Are these qualities they inspire and reinforce

Traditional Aesthetics in BWV 1068#

  • Voice Leading: Bach’s voice leading is impeccable, creating smooth transitions between chords and modes. This craftsmanship ensures that even when the harmony becomes complex, it never feels jarring.

  • Mode Shifts: The move from Ionian to Dorian and back adds a layer of narrative to the music. This shift can evoke a sense of exploration and return, a journey that resonates deeply with listeners who appreciate traditional harmonic storytelling.

  • Emotional Impact: The use of traditional harmonic progressions to evoke and resolve tension can be more emotionally impactful if you value clarity and structure in music.

Your appreciation for the traditional aesthetics and the harmonic journey in BWV 1068 highlights a preference for music that balances complexity with resolution. This balance, masterfully executed by Bach, provides a sense of emotional and structural completeness that can be incredibly satisfying.

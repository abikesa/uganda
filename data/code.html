Synthesis#

Art, Science, Morality#

Let’s pick a leaf with regard to the “products” of academic research: Papers with embedded figures vs. Apps embedded in a Jupyter Book? Static vs. Dynamic? Final vs. Iterative? Nietzsche’s aphoristic style reflects his philosophy perfectly—disjointed, sharp, and meant to provoke rather than resolve. It’s fitting for someone who rejects linear narratives and grand teleological conclusions, as you pointed out with Marx and Dostoevsky. His idea of eternal recurrence, that life endlessly repeats without progress or redemption, clashes with the arcs that Dostoevsky’s tortured characters or Marx’s historical materialism offer. Those two are deeply invested in narrative arcs: Dostoevsky in the psychological and spiritual, Marx in the socio-political. Nietzsche, by contrast, thrives in fragmentation and repetition—the absence of a clean resolution is the point. It mirrors life’s chaotic, non-linear nature.

He’s like a philosopher who enjoys the struggle but isn’t interested in wrapping it up with a bow. His style keeps the tension alive, which is his strength.
